BRAND & DESIGN SYSTEM

The LA Global identity.

A reference for how the brand looks, sounds, and behaves across the site and future materials — decks, signage, social, and beyond.

MARK

A convergence point.

The mark is a globe intersected by an ascending line — three strokes converging to a single point, standing in for Media, Technology, and Capital meeting in one engagement. The amber node marks where they connect.

COLOR

A confident, restrained palette.

Ink and white carry most of the design. Cobalt is the primary action color — links, buttons, key data. Amber is used sparingly, as a signal: tags, the logo node, accents in dark sections.

VOICE

How LA Global sounds.

01

Direct

Short, declarative sentences. No filler, no hedging. We say what we mean and back it with specifics.

02

Operator-grade

We write like people who've run businesses, not like a marketing team describing one. Concrete nouns over abstract buzzwords.

03

Confident, not loud

Authority comes from clarity and specificity, not superlatives. We rarely say "best-in-class" — we say what we actually do.

04

Global register

Written for an international audience — clear, unidiomatic English; numbers and stage names (Seed, Series A) used precisely.
